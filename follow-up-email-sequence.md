**Clause 2: "Emergency Service Available at 50% Premium"**
Translation: If your tank goes empty or you need same-day delivery, the base service cost jumps 50%. This happens more than you'd think.  
Cost Impact: $[500–2,000]/year in emergency overages

**Clause 3: "Pricing in USD; Customer Absorbs Currency Fluctuation"**
Translation: If the US dollar strengthens, your bill goes up automatically. No protection against exchange risk.  
Cost Impact: $[200–800]/year depending on currency moves

Cost Impact: $[300–600]/month in unused capacity

Translation: On top of the base service, you're charged for the physical exchange of your tank. These small fees ($[25–75] per swap) add hundreds annually.

## Implementation Notes

### Personalization Checklist

Before sending any email, customize:
- [ ] Prospect name
- [ ] Company name
- [ ] Industry (pharmaceutical, manufacturing, hospital, lab, etc.)
- [ ] Estimated facility size/capacity
- [ ] Known provider name (if you've researched them)
- [ ] Relevant location references
- [ ] Any known compliance or uptime concerns

### Timing & Cadence

- **Email 1 (Problem Hook):** Day 1 (cold outreach or new contact)
- **Email 2 (Education + Audit):** Day 7–10 (if no reply or soft reply)
- **Email 3 (Case Study):** Day 14–17 (if still engaged)
- **Email 4 (Urgency):** Day 21–28 (if very engaged but no action)
- **Email 5 (Final):** Day 35+ (soft close)

### What Triggers Advancement to Next Email

- **Move to Email 2** if: No reply to Email 1, OR reply asking for more info
- **Move to Email 3** if: Engaged but no contract submission yet
- **Move to Email 4** if: Still talking but hesitant to commit
- **Move to Email 5** if: Multiple touches with no response (don't be pushy—this is a soft goodbye)

### What Should Trigger a Sales Call Instead

Stop the email sequence and schedule a call if:
- They submit a contract for analysis
- They ask specific pricing questions
- They mention timeframe for decision
- They ask about service details or capabilities
