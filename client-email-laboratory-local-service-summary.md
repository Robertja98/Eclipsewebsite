Subject: A local DI service model can reduce risk for your lab

Hello [Client Name],

For laboratories, deionized and high purity water service is not just a supply issue. It directly affects test consistency, rinse quality, instrument support, and day-to-day operational reliability.

A local Ontario service model can help reduce risk and cost by limiting:

- Long transportation loops that increase delay exposure
- Customs brokerage and cross-border administration
- U.S. dollar pricing volatility
- Emergency freight costs when a tank change is urgent
- The need to hold extra spare tanks as a contingency

For laboratory environments, local support also improves:

- Response time when service demand changes unexpectedly
- Coordination around sensitive schedules and uptime requirements
- Technical follow-up when water quality drifts or demand changes
- Confidence that service intervals reflect actual usage patterns

In many cases, the savings are not only in freight. They come from fewer disruptions, fewer workaround costs, and more predictable support for high purity water demand.

If useful, we can review your current DI or high purity water service approach and identify areas where local support may improve cost control and response reliability.
