Many facilities look only at the quoted service price for DI water support, but the real cost of service is often higher when tanks, regeneration cycles, or replacement logistics depend on a cross-border model.

A local Ontario service model can help reduce total landed cost by limiting:

- Longer freight routes and added handling
- Customs brokerage and border administration
- Exposure to U.S. dollar pricing and exchange-rate swings
- Emergency shipping costs when schedules change
- The need for extra spare tanks or service buffers

Local support can also improve service quality and response time.

For facilities that depend on deionized or high purity water, proximity matters because it can make it easier to:

- Respond faster to urgent tank changes or service issues
- Maintain more predictable water quality support
- Align service intervals with actual operating schedules
- Reduce downtime risk tied to logistics delays

In practice, the savings are often not just freight savings. They come from fewer disruptions, fewer contingency costs, and better schedule reliability.

If it would be useful, we can review your current service model and identify where avoidable cost or operational risk may be built into it.
